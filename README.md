## Overview
The **NSF - IAIFI PhD Summer School 2026** is an intensive, interdisciplinary program focused on the intersection of **Artificial Intelligence and Fundamental Physics**. Organized by the **Institute for Artificial Intelligence and Fundamental Interactions (IAIFI)**, the summer school brings together leading researchers, early-career scientists, and students to explore cutting-edge AI methods applied to physics research.

The program combines foundational lectures, hands-on tutorials, collaborative hackathon projects, and networking opportunities with experts from academia and research institutions worldwide.

---

## Mission
The mission of the IAIFI PhD Summer School is to leverage the expertise of IAIFI researchers, affiliates, and partners to promote **education, interdisciplinary research, and workforce development** at the intersection of AI and Physics.

---

## Dates & Location
- **Dates:** August 3–7, 2026  
- **Location:** University of Massachusetts, Boston  
- **Format:** In-person and Virtual Attendance  
- **Followed by:** IAIFI Summer Workshop (open to researchers at all career stages)

## About IAIFI
The **Institute for Artificial Intelligence and Fundamental Interactions (IAIFI)** advances physics discoveries and foundational AI research by developing novel AI approaches grounded in:
- First principles of physics  
- Best practices in machine learning  
- Domain knowledge from fundamental sciences  

The Summer School highlights interdisciplinary research, encourages global collaboration, and supports practical learning through tutorials and hackathon projects.

---

## Program Structure
The Summer School includes:
- Expert-led lectures
- Hands-on, code-based tutorials
- A collaborative hackathon project
- Career and networking events
- Lightning talks and breakout sessions
